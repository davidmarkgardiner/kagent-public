Stakeholder presentation · agentic triage / CHAIR

Stop rebuilding the evidence after the alert.

After approximately six months of attempts to make the LGTM/Alertmanager route deliver actionable evidence to the triage system, it has not become a reliable operational path. The decision is to stop making this use case dependent on it.

Decision: LGTM/Alertmanager is not fit for purpose as the primary log/event evidence transport for this triage system. Retain it for human metric paging, dashboards and search; use Alloy → Vector → Kafka for agent-triggering evidence.

Fleet placement: local processing, central triage

Boundary: each worker Vector prepares the evidence locally; it does not call the agent or hold GitLab credentials. Kafka is the worker-to-management handoff. Management alone validates, claims, invokes Argo/kagent and creates tickets.

Two flows, very different failure surfaces

Old route · repeatedly unable to meet the triage need

Alert abstraction first

  • Evidence is no longer the primary payload.
  • Needs custom conversion/proxy ownership.
  • Can depend on further lookup to explain the alert.
  • Repeated delivery/support dependency has not produced a dependable triage path.
Recommended route · evidence first

Evidence package first

  • Pod, cluster, namespace, event and log evidence travel together.
  • Noise reduction and data minimisation occur before egress.
  • Management owns durable dedupe, policy, triage and ticket audit.
  • Grafana becomes optional follow-up, not a first-diagnosis dependency.

What changes—and what does not

We stop doing

Building our agent-evidence route around additional Alertmanager-facing proxy/webhook conversion and waiting for external capacity to make that path usable.

We keep

Alertmanager for human metric paging, grouping, silences and escalation. Grafana/Loki for dashboards, search and deeper investigation.

We prove next

A reversible one-worker, non-production pilot with fleet-grade redaction, durable failure-safe idempotency, quarantine, replay and ticket-retry controls.

What the proof already establishes

Actual log → ticketActual BackOff event → ticketRead-only agentIn-window replay suppression

Important: this is a single-cluster proof, not a production fleet claim. Fable’s critique identified the controls required before office rollout: workload/signature fingerprints, durable claim failure handling, redaction rule pack/scanner, quarantine/DLQ, safe replay, ticket idempotency and fleet-grade worker buffering.

Requested stakeholder decision

Approve the evidence-first direction and a parallel, non-production one-worker pilot once the named critique controls are accepted. Do not change existing human Alertmanager routes as part of that pilot.
